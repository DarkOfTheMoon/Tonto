What can TONTO do?

This depends on whether you are a user or a programmer.

As a user, you can do the following types of calculations:

  • Hartree-Fock molecular calculations of all types except general restricted Hartree-Fock, using gaussian basis sets.
  • Density plots of various kinds: charge, spin, orbital, current, solenoidal current, irrotational current, electric potential, and ELF density plots, Fermi mobility function plots for all types of gaussian basis sets, density matrices.
  • Vibrationally averaged density plots, as above, for diatomics.
  • Mulliken and Roby population analyses.
  • Decomposition of the SCF energy into orbital and orbital pair contributions.
  • Elastic X-ray and PND scattering intensities calculated from non-interacting Hartree-Fock molecular wavefunctions in any spacegroup.
  • Elastic X-ray and PND scattering intensity statistics, such as qq-plots, and angle / intensity dependence of the z deviation.
  • Non-interacting Hartree-Fock molecular wavefunctions fitted to X-ray and PND scattering data in any spacegroup.

As a programmer, you have html access to comprehensive online documentation for every module. The modules available define types which deal with the following issues:

  • Error handling, memory management, call-stack management, and timing.
  • Basic type (integer, real, and string) manipulation.
  • Vectors, matrices, and multidimensional arrays of all types.
  • Binary, text, and archive files.
  • Gaussian basis functions, shells, shell pairs and quartets, basis sets, including integrals.
  • Rectilinear grids.
  • Pointgroups symmetry including irreps.
  • Crystal (spacegroup) symmetry and unit cells.
  • DIIS extrapolation for vectors and matrices.
  • Atoms and molecules.
