How do we achieve the aims of TONTO?

To achieve the goal of simplicity means that the logical structure of TONTO must be clearly expressed and divided into self contained units. Any changes that might be required for TONTO are then performed on isolated logical sub-units which do not disrupt the program as a whole. A by-product of such an approach is that TONTO can easily evolve to cope with, and take advantage of, the rapid changes in computer hardware and software that are the norm today.

Of course, the design of a program into logically separate subunits is a general requirement for any programming task. The most significant choice that affects the logical design of the program, and its efficiency, (i.e. the two stated goals) is the choice of programming launguage. For numerical applications, there are really only two possibilities which are popular enough to be considered--Fortran95 or C/C++.

In the next section the choices are compared, but to cut a long story short, we used Fortran coupled with a preprocessor. It's simpler and more robust than C++.
